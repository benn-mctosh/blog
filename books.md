The purpose and shape of this blog are ever-changing, but one thing I do is read and talk about books—here's a running list of some of those I've devoted particular attention to.
## Data and other technology

**Ann Blair's [_Too Much to Know_](/2023/04/11/the-dangers-of-encyclopedias/#the-book-something-different) (2010):** _What can we learn about generative AI from the discomfort and anxiety many early modern scholars felt about the rise of reference genres like encyclopedias and florilegia?_

**Meghan O'Gieblyn's [_God, Human, Animal, Machine_](/2023/06/03/god-human-animal-machine/#the-book) (2022):** _Why do hardline Calvinist eschatology and transhumanism's apocalyptic visions of the singularity have so much in common? How can we escape this bleak vision of consciousness that leaves little room for human freedom?_

**Jacob Soll's [_The Information Master_](/2023/01/30/statement-of-purpose/#the-book) (2011):** _How did Jean-Baptiste Colbert build a very modern-looking information bureaucracy and surveillance state, before "bureaucracy" was even coined?_

*Not strictly a review of* **Nate Silver's *[On the Edge](/2025/01/28/what-pluralism-on-ai-could-actually-look-like)* (2024)** *but instead responding Nate's use of his own "The Village/The River" framework to generate a blinkered picture of how people are talking about AI, or at least were eons ago (early 2025).*

## History of biology

**Gerald Gieson's [_The Private Science of Louis Pasteur_](/2023/03/21/pasteur-private-science-geison/#the-book) (1995):** _What can famed bacteriologist Louis Pasteur's lab notebooks tell us about the role of myth-making and outright deceit in scientific discovery?_

**Hannah Landecker's [_Culturing Life_](/2023/06/21/culturing-life-vat-grown-lard/#why-cultivated-meat) (2010):** _Inspired by a visit to a cultured-meat start-up, I turned to Landecker to think about some forerunners to the changes such ventures hope to induce in our very concepts of food, life, and animality._

**Banu Subramaniam's [_Holy Science_](/2023/07/27/holy-science-hindu-nationalism-banu-subramaniam/#the-book) (2019):** _What does it mean that Hindu nationalists have embraced scientific modernity while claiming to connect it to ancient tradition? Are there more liberatory possibilities for this sort of "archaic modernity"?_

## History of the social sciences

**Elizabeth Popp Berman's [_Thinking Like an Economist_](/2023/02/10/against-efficiency/#the-book) (2022):** _How did efficiency, rather than any other moral principle, become the yardstick against which American policy is measured? What does that mean for our ability to govern ourselves?_

**Sarah Igo's [_The Averaged Americans_](/2023/02/28/averaging-america/#the-book) (2008):** _How did Americans come to see themselves–as people, and as a nation–as something knowable through opinion polling?_

**Fernando Vidal's [_The Sciences of the Soul_](/2023/02/14/the-foundational-science/#psychology-the-first-and-most-useful-science) (2011):** _Psychology means the study of the psyche, the soul—what does taking that literally tell us about the field's origin and central place in Enlightenment knowledge?_
